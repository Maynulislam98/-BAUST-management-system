about us

Brief History of BAUST

BAUST is a private university sponsored by Bangladesh Army Welfare Trust. Bangladesh Army established BAUST on 15 Feb 2015 at Saidpur Cantonment and it was inaugurated by Asaduzzaman Noor, MP, Hon’ble Minister of Cultural Affairs of Bangladesh on the same date.
The intention to establish BAUST is to spread technical education to the remote places of the country. BAUST got approval to start its academic activities from Ministry of Education and UGC on 17 February 2015 and launched its academic session with four departments (CSE, EEE, ME, and A & S) on 15 Feb 2015 under 03 faculties. At present BAUST is functioning with 07 departments (CSE, EEE, ME, IPE, CE, BBA, English and A&S) under 05 faculties.

BAUST is in full compliance with the Private University Act 2010 and instructions issued by Army Welfare Trust, Ministry of Education and University Grants Commission of Bangladesh.

Facilities

MODERN LIBRARY

Bangladesh Army University of Science and Technology (BAUST) is only a private Science and Technology University for north region of the country. The library is called the heart of an institution. University creates knowledge through the research and the library of the university preserves the all kind of research results (knowledge). So the library has been playing an enormous role for conducting research, lesson preparing and learning. The library hosts a vast and diverse collection of books, journals, monograms and periodicals of academic interest.

QUALIFIED TEACHER

Teachers not only listen, but also coach and mentor their students. We have many more PhD and foreign degree holder qualified faculties. They are able to help shape academic goals and are dedicated to getting their students to achieve them. Teachers have patience for their students and are understanding when a concept isn't taking.

HALL FACILITIES

The university believes that campus life is an important aspect in the development process of students. The university has three residential halls for the accommodations of the students. Abbas Uddin Ahmed Hall (AUAH): The hall for the male students is named after the famous Bhawaiya folk song composer and singer of Rangpur Abbas Uddin Ahmed. This hall has 515 seat capacities. Bir Protik Taramon Bibi Hall (BPTBH): The hall for the female students is named after Bir Protik Taramon Bibi who is one of the two female freedom fighters in Bangladesh obtaining the Bir Protik award. This hall has 470 seat capacities. New Hall: This is the hall for the male students. This hall has 300 seat capacities.

WI-FI CAMPUS

Internet has become an essential part of our modern life. Internet finds more importance in the case of an engineering student. BAUST is committed to make our students familiar with modern technologies and recent world. That is why we have provided WIFI facility in our campus and in both the halls. Each student, faculty member and stuffs of BAUST can enjoy high speed internet facility 24/7 inside of the BAUST campus or while remaining in his/her own room in the halls.
